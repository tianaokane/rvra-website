Our Story

Take a walk through the history of RVRA — from a small community idea to a vibrant centre of connection, learning, and change.

1998

Founded in Limavady

RVRA began as a grassroots group of residents coming together to support local needs and youth development.

2006

New Community House Opens

We opened our first permanent space — the Community House on Keady Way — providing meeting rooms and space for activities.

2015

Community Hub Launched

We expanded to Catherine Street, opening the Community Hub for wider programming and inclusive events.

2020

Adapting During COVID

We stayed connected through remote services, doorstep drop-ins, and virtual youth programming to support residents in isolation.

Today

Still Growing, Still Listening

Now a trusted centre in Limavady, we continue to deliver inclusive, creative, and responsive community support for all ages.

It Wouldn't Have Been Possible Without Our Team

Behind every programme, event, and act of support is a passionate, dedicated team of people who care deeply about this community.
